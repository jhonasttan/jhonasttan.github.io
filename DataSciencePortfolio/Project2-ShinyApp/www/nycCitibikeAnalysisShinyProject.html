What is the Problem?

As a non subscriber of NYC , I have been fascinated with the number of people riding bikes daily in NYC to and from work. I asked a few citibike subscribers about their experience, and yes, some include dealing with a pothole or two; therefore, was interested in the logistics. My experience with the NYC Citibike Station Map website led me to think about how do users determine if there will be bikes at a given station, and will there be enough bike docks at their destination? The site's station locate feature allows you to enter one address, with an instant popup on the map displaying the number of available bikes and docks. However, I felt this point was only the start. A feature that allows me to determine bike / dock availability at the station / destination with trip duration would allow me to plan my route, with possible routing alternatives, if the duration of the trip may result in a surcharge.  I spoke with a few members of my cohort, my  instructor Chris and some external peers to confirm the pursuit of a solution for this case was worthwhile.

My cohort peer referred me to a great article that was mentioned by a guest speaker (Andy Eschobar) that provided a detailed analysis on Citibike ridership.
